About Us

The Veronica Anthony Foundation is dedicated to creating lasting positive change in communities through climate action, education, health initiatives, and sustainable development.

Our Mission

To promote sustainable development by empowering individuals and communities through innovative programs in education, climate action, clean energy, health, and livelihood enhancement. We are committed to fostering self-reliance, reducing inequalities, and creating resilient systems that protect the environment while improving quality of life.

Our Vision

To build empowered individuals, thriving communities, and a sustainable environment that collectively ensures a brighter future for generations to come.

Our Impact

Making a difference in communities through measurable results and sustainable change

Our Journey So Far

Women & Youth Empowerment

We have successfully trained over 1,000 women and youth in entrepreneurship, digital skills, and sustainable agriculture practices, leading to the establishment of 50+ small-scale businesses in local communities.

Climate Action & Clean Energy

We have implemented renewable energy solutions in 25 communities, providing clean cooking facilities and solar-powered lighting to over 500 households, reducing carbon emissions and improving health conditions.

Education & Healthcare

We have established educational support programs reaching 2,000 students, provided healthcare awareness sessions to 30 communities, and facilitated medical outreach programs benefiting over 5,000 individuals.

Our Vision for

Expanding Community Reach

We aim to extend our programs to 20 additional communities, focusing on rural areas with limited access to basic amenities and educational resources. Our goal is to create sustainable impact through long-term community engagement.

Sustainable Development Initiatives

We plan to launch 10 new climate action projects, including community gardens, waste management systems, and renewable energy installations. We aim to reduce carbon footprint while creating economic opportunities.

Enhanced Education & Healthcare

We plan to set up digital learning centers in 15 communities, provide scholarships to 100 underprivileged students, and organize monthly health camps focusing on preventive healthcare and nutrition education.

Cook Clean Advocate Training 2.0

Become a clean cooking advocate

A capacity-building program for youth, community volunteers, and young leaders across Africa—building advocacy skills to accelerate safe, affordable, and sustainable cooking solutions where traditional fuels still harm health, the environment, and livelihoods.

Starts March 27, 2026 — weekly sessions on Fridays, 11:00 AM WAT (two hours per session, including a short break).
Cohorts planned up to three times per year; expert facilitators from enterprises, governments, UN and regional energy centres, CSOs, and climate advocates across Africa.
Certificate of completion for eligible participants; top advocates may qualify for internship, volunteer, or mentorship placements with partners.

Program focus

  • Advocacy and clean cooking technologies; health and environmental impacts of household air pollution
  • Grassroots campaigns, school outreach, policy dialogue, and market linkages
  • Gender equity and engagement with policymakers and stakeholders

Partners help deliver mentors, placements, and resources toward a goal of growing a strong network of trained clean cooking advocates continent-wide.

Innovation challenge AICCEES 2026

Africa International Conference on Clean Energy & Energy Storage

AICCEES 2026 Clean Energy Innovation Challenge

Pan-African call for innovators building solutions across energy access, renewables, storage, clean cooking, mobility, circular economy, climate resilience, and transition fuels—with mentorship, pitch exposure at AICCEES 2026, and pathways to funding and partnerships.

Applications open 1 April 2026
Shortlisting 31 August 2026
Mentorship bootcamp 1–20 September 2026
Live pitch 24–25 September 2026 (during AICCEES)
Apply for the challenge Visit aiccees.com

Innovators retain full ownership of IP; no equity required to participate; confidential information is protected throughout the process.

Programme structure, focus areas & eligibility

Four stages

  1. Call for innovators — Open applications across Africa via the AICCEES website.
  2. Screening & shortlisting — Independent technical review; 10–15 high-potential finalists.
  3. Mentorship bootcamp (pre-conference) — Pitch development, market readiness, business and impact models.
  4. Live pitch session — Five-minute pitches plus Q&A during the Clean Energy Business Innovation & Mentorship Session at AICCEES 2026.

Innovation themes

  • Energy access and off-grid solutions
  • Renewable energy systems and productive use
  • Clean cooking technologies and fuels
  • Energy storage, smart grids, and digital energy solutions
  • Sustainable mobility and clean transport
  • Waste-to-energy and circular economy solutions
  • Climate adaptation and resilience technologies
  • Hydrogen, biofuels, and clean transition fuels

Who can apply

  • African-led startups, SMEs, and scale-ups
  • Research institutions and university spin-offs
  • Teams at prototype, pilot, or early commercial stage
  • Women-led and youth-led clean energy enterprises

Awards & benefits

  • Proof-of-concept or seed funding support
  • Sponsored incubation or accelerator placements
  • Business mentorship, investor introductions, and media exposure
  • Conference recognition and feature in the AICCEES Innovation Showcase publication

For enquiries, participation, and sponsorship: info@toveroenergy.com

Start-a-Skill Program

A virtual skills series helping Nigerian youth close the gap between classroom learning and what employers expect—through hands-on sessions in Microsoft Office, Google Workspace, digital collaboration, AI productivity tools, freelancing platforms, and career readiness (CVs, interviews, LinkedIn, and more).

Program objectives

  • Bridge the digital skills gap with practical, market-ready tools—on a pathway to train 1,000 youth over two years.
  • Improve workplace readiness and professional branding so graduates meet employer expectations.
  • Build confidence for the digital economy, including freelancing and professional networking.
